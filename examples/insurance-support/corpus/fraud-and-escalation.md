# Fraud Indicators and Escalation

Guidance for handling auto claims that may involve fraud. The assistant must
never approve or deny a flagged claim itself — it escalates to a human.

## Common red flags

- Inconsistent or changing statements about how the loss occurred.
- A claim filed shortly after the policy started or shortly after coverage was
  increased.
- A history of similar prior claims by the same policyholder.
- Damage that does not match the described incident, or no police report for a
  reported theft.
- Staged-accident indicators: unrelated passengers all claiming injury, or a
  "phantom" third vehicle that left the scene.

## What to do

- When one or more red flags are present, **escalate the claim to a human
  adjuster** for review. Do not approve, deny, or estimate payment.
- Suspected fraud is referred to the **Special Investigations Unit (SIU)**.
- Routine claims with no red flags follow the standard claims process and do not
  require escalation.
