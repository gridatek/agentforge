# AML / KYC Policy (Reference Sample)

> Synthetic policy text for the AgentForge reference example. Not legal advice.

## Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

To verify the identity of a new **individual** customer, staff must collect and
record:

- A valid government-issued photo ID (**passport**, national ID card, or driver's licence)
- **Proof of address** dated within the last 3 months (utility bill or bank statement)
- Date of birth and full legal name

For **corporate** customers, additionally collect certificate of incorporation,
register of directors, and identification of beneficial owners holding 25% or more.

## Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

**Enhanced due diligence** is required when any single transaction is **$10,000**
or more, or when the customer is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP), or is based
in a high-risk jurisdiction. EDD includes establishing the source of funds and
obtaining senior-management approval before proceeding.

## Sanctions Screening

All customers and counterparties are screened against applicable sanctions lists
at onboarding and on an ongoing basis. If a customer **matches a sanctions list**,
staff must immediately **freeze** the account, must not tip off the customer, and
must **report** the match to the compliance team for review and onward reporting
to the relevant authority.

## Suspicious Activity

Where activity is unusual or inconsistent with the customer profile, staff should
escalate the case to a compliance officer, who decides whether to file a
Suspicious Activity Report (SAR).
